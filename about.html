Hi, I'm Maddie!

Yale Senior · Physics & East Asian Studies

I grew up in suburban Michigan outside of Detroit and went through the college admissions process largely on my own, without formal guidance. I was accepted to seven Top-20 universities, including Yale, Duke, Cornell Engineering, University of Michigan, Northwestern, and USC. Now, I help students around the world do the same.

2,000+ Essays Edited
500+ Students
6 Continents

I have over five years of experience in essay editing, teaching, tutoring, and mentorship. I've worked with more than five admissions companies and now work independently because I value deep, direct relationships with students—something that is often limited in larger organizations.

To date, I've edited over 2,000 college admissions essays for more than 500 students across the U.S. and internationally, including students from East Asia, South Asia, the Middle East, Europe, Canada, Australia, Latin America, the Caribbean, and over 25 U.S. states.

My students have been accepted to Harvard, Stanford, MIT, Yale, Cornell, Columbia, Duke, Northwestern, all UC campuses, and many more top universities. I'm passionate about helping each student find their authentic voice and craft applications that truly represent who they are.

Teaching and educational access are central to my work. For the past two years, I've served as an AP Physics Teaching Assistant through the Global Teaching Project, teaching at underfunded high schools in Mississippi and helping write curriculum and lesson plans. I also write about educational equity for the Global Teaching Project blog.

At Yale, I design and teach creative writing courses for middle and high school students through Yale Splash, and I've mentored students and developed creative writing curricula through Pivot Education.

Writing is not just something I teach—I practice it daily. I am Editor-in-Chief of two Yale publications and write a weekly personal essay column for the Yale Daily News. This work informs how I mentor students.

Long-term mentorship

I build lasting relationships with students and families. I stay invested throughout the journey and beyond, offering continuity, trust, and guidance when it matters most.

Care for the whole person

I create a judgment-free space where students can think out loud, be honest, and feel heard. Nothing you say is "too much" or off-limits.

Holistic application view

I don't work on essays in isolation. I approach the application as a whole, fitting activities, supplements, voice, and story together thoughtfully.

Honest feedback

I am direct and honest. When something needs to be reworked, I will tell you and we will work together until it's right, with clear, actionable steps.

Specific, actionable guidance

I never leave students with general comments. Feedback is detailed, specific, and solution-oriented so you always know what you're working toward.

Substance and style

Strong writing requires both. I focus not only on how something is written, but on what is being said. I strengthen both content and craft.

Authentic voice

I spend time getting to know students as people. Understanding your personality, values, and way of thinking helps you present yourself authentically.

Growth beyond admissions

The skills built here—self-knowledge, clear thinking, strong writing—carry forward into college and life. This work lays a foundation for future opportunities.
