I'm a senior at Yale University majoring in Physics and East Asian Studies. I grew up in suburban Michigan outside of Detroit and went through the college admissions process largely on my own, without formal guidance. I applied strategically and was accepted to seven Top-20 universities, including Yale, Duke, Cornell Engineering, the University of Michigan (Engineering, Early Action), Notre Dame (Early Action), USC, Northwestern, and Michigan State's Honors College.

I have over five years of experience in essay editing, teaching, tutoring, and mentorship. I've worked with more than five admissions companies and now work independently because I value deep, direct relationships with students—something that is often limited in larger organizations.

To date, I've edited over 2,000 college admissions essays for more than 500 students across the U.S. and internationally, including students from East Asia, South Asia, the Middle East, Europe, Canada, Australia, Latin America, the Caribbean, and over 25 U.S. states.

Teaching & Educational Access

Teaching and educational access are central to my work. For the past two years, I've served as an AP Physics Teaching Assistant through the Global Teaching Project, teaching at underfunded high schools in Mississippi and helping write curriculum and lesson plans. I also write about educational equity for the Global Teaching Project blog.

At Yale, I design and teach creative writing courses for middle and high school students through Yale Splash, and I've mentored students and developed creative writing curricula through Pivot Education.

Lastly, writing is not just something I teach. I practice writing daily. I am Editor-in-Chief of two Yale publications and write a weekly personal essay column for the Yale Daily News. This work informs how I mentor students.

My Values

I build lasting relationships with students and families. College admissions is not a one-off task. Rather, it's a formative process. I stay invested throughout the journey and beyond, offering continuity, trust, and guidance when it matters most.

The admissions process is stressful, emotional, and deeply personal. I create a judgment-free space where students can think out loud, be honest, and feel heard. Nothing you say is "too much" or off-limits. I am here to listen and support you through the full experience.

I don't work on essays in isolation. Every part of the application connects: activities, supplements, voice, structure, and story. I approach the application as a whole, fitting the pieces together thoughtfully so everything works in harmony.

I am direct and honest in my feedback. When something needs to be reworked, I will tell you and we will work together until it's right. Critique is never vague or discouraging. Every piece of feedback comes with clear, actionable steps and a concrete plan forward.

I never leave students with general comments or uncertainty. Feedback is detailed, specific, and solution-oriented. My students and I form a team. We set clear goals, track progress, and adjust strategy as needed so students always know what they're working toward and why.

Strong writing requires both. I focus not only on how something is written, but on what is being said. Ideas, arguments, and insight matter just as much as polish. I strengthen both content and craft.

I spend time getting to know students as people through conversation, reflection, and trust. Understanding your personality, values, passions, and way of thinking allows us to help you present yourself authentically and confidently.

College admissions is a beginning, not an endpoint. The skills built here—self-knowledge, clear thinking, strong writing, and confidence—carry forward into college and life beyond it. This work lays a foundation for future opportunities, not just acceptances.
